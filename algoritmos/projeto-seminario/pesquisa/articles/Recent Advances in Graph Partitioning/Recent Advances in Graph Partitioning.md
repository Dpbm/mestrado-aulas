## ideas


- A hypergraph H = (V, E) is a generalization of a graph in which an edge (usually called hy-
peredge or net) can connect any number of nodes. As with graphs, partitioning a hypergraph
also means to find an assignment of nodes to different blocks of (mostly) equal size. The
objective function, however, is usually expressed differently. A straightforward generaliza-
tion of the edge cut to hypergraphs is the hyperedge cut. It counts the number of hyperedges
that connect different blocks.
